# Behavioral Onboarding Case Studies

Use these case studies as references when designing onboarding loops.

---

## Case Study A: Duolingo (The Master of Progressive Investment)

Duolingo uses a hybrid onboarding model that leans heavily on the **IKEA Effect** and **Endowed Progress**.

```
Select Language ➔ Choose Daily Goal ➔ Select Starting Level ➔ Quick Lesson (Aha! Moment) ➔ Investment (Create Profile)
```

### Psychological Levers Used:
1. **Interactive Sandbox First:** Users start learning a language within 10 seconds of opening the app. There is no sign-up wall.
2. **IKEA Effect / Labor:** Users invest labor by completing a 2-minute quick lesson. By the end of it, they have earned points (XP) and established a streak (Day 1).
3. **Endowed Progress:** The app shows progress bars filling up during the lesson, celebrating with mascot animations (Duo).
4. **Delayed & Gated Investment:** The user is only prompted to create an account at the very end to "save their progress and streak." The threat of losing their newly acquired streak (Sunk Cost Fallacy) makes sign-up conversion extremely high.

## Case Study B: FitBite Pro / Health Apps (IKEA Effect & Paywall Optimization)

Fitness and subscription-based health apps use a long onboarding structure to justify premium prices.

```
Goal Quiz ➔ Demographic Survey ➔ Personalization Loader ➔ Variable Reward (My Plan) ➔ Paywall ➔ Reminders (Investment)
```

### Psychological Levers Used:
1. **Internal Trigger Activation:** Questions focus on deep frustrations ("How long have you struggled with weight?", "Do you feel low on energy?").
2. **High Labor Investment (IKEA Effect):** 15-20 highly relevant questions. The labor spent makes the user believe the app's output is scientifically tailored to them.
3. **Variable Reward (Endowed Progress & Payoff):** A custom loader screen showing "Analyzing metabolic rate... Creating meal plan..." creates anticipation. It ends by showing a personalized milestone chart (e.g., "You will reach your target weight on October 10").
4. **Sunk Cost Paywall:** The subscription paywall is shown right after the milestone chart. Users are highly motivated to buy because they have already spent 4 minutes setting it up and want the promised results.
5. **Day 2 Trigger Hook:** Immediately after the paywall/home screen, users select their preferred reminder time, setting up the exact push notification prompt that will wake them up on Day 2.
